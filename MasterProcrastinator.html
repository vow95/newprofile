Tim talks about his "psychological deficiencies" on his blog Wait But Why. He's also a co-founder of ArborBridge, a company that provides educational services.

The Procrastinator's Methodology

Tim wanted to explain what happens in procrastinators' thoughts.As a result, he devised a three-character structure to depict the procrastinator's system:
The Decisive Rationalist
Monkey of Instant Gratification
The Monster of Panic


The Decisive Rationalist

The Sensible Decision-Maker makes a rational decision to complete a task. He wants you to consider the facts and do whatever makes the most sense right now. The Rational Decision-Maker empowers humans to do things that no other animal can:

  • Consider the future
  • Look at the big picture
  • Plan ahead of time

    • The Monkey of Instant Gratification

    Immediate gratification Monkey is completely absorbed in the current moment. He has no recollection of the past or the future, and he just cares about two things: simple and fun. The Monkey was useful in tribal times. It is concerned with sleeping, feeding, and reproducing. However, the Monkey fails us in sophisticated civilisation. It makes us postpone doing the difficult tasks that are necessary to live a meaningful life.



  • Conflict and Agreement

    • It makes logical sense to do things that are simple and enjoyable, which leads to the Rational Decision Maker and the Monkey agreeing, resulting in amazing play and good times. However, there are times when it makes sense to complete more difficult and less enjoyable tasks; this condition produces conflict, and the Monkey frequently prevails; when the Monkey wins, we postpone.     The Monster of Panic

    The Panic Monster is mostly dormant, although he can awaken at any time: a deadline approaches too quickly, or There is a risk of public humiliation.

    The Monkey is afraid of the Panic Monster, so when it appears, the Monkey relinquishes power to the Rationional Decision-Maker. There are two types of procrastination.

    Procrastination may be divided into two types:

  • Procrastination on time-sensitive tasks
  • Procrastination on difficult tasks with no deadlines
    • Because the Panic Monster becomes engaged when deadlines approach, we naturally cope with the first form of procrastination. The repercussions of procrastination are short-term
    The second sort of procrastination, on the other hand, is harmful since there are no deadlines, thus the Panic Monster is never aroused, and we are free to postpone indefinitely.
    Worse, the second type is frequently less evident, as it is generally experienced discreetly and secretly, resulting in long-term misery and regret. The second sort of procrastination affects us all.





    If you're an entrepreneur, there are no deadlines at first, and nothing happens (unless you go out and do the hard work to start things moving). Personal instances of things that don't have deadlines are:

  • Seeing your relatives
  • Exercising
  • Good nutrition
  • Creating new connections
  • The ultimate deadline is your life


    • Keep in mind that your time on this earth is limited, and set deadlines for yourself even if they aren't set by others.
