KINTSUGI.. GOLDEN REPAIR

  • Post On16.6.22
  • ByAhad Mjaddami

When a bowl is broken in Japan, it is reassembled with the fractures filled with gold to create a beautiful lining. Kintsugi (金継ぎ, きんつぎ, “golden joinery” ), also known as Kintsukuroi (金繕い, きんつくろい, “golden repair”), is the process of restoring broken bowls with gold. This is done to highlight the beauty of something that was once broken.

They believe that when something has been damaged and has a past, it becomes more beautiful; the same goes for human beings. Everything you've gone through, and everything you're going through, does not make your life uglier, even if it may appear that way when we're in the middle of it. It is up to us to decide whether or not to paint our difficulties in gold and make them beautiful.

You are not irreparably damaged !!
You can pick yourself up and learn from what's happened, and as a result of your battle, you can become a better person; you can proudly wear scars like a badge of honor, as if to say, "Look at what I've been through, it's made me who I am today." I'm confident that I can handle whatever life throws at me now.

It's OKAY

Nobody has ever had or will ever have a flawless existence. It's entirely up to us whether we choose to make our broken pieces beautiful by painting them gold.

Don't be ashamed of what's going on with you. Everything that has happened to you has happened for a reason. As a result, the more we deny, grumble, and refuse to accept what has happened to us, the less beneficial it becomes. It's like painting the gaps in our shattered pieces of gold, changing something that may be ugly into something beautiful and inspiring, the instant we receive and find what's beneficial in the struggle, the things we've been through.



All worth it

When what you have been through is an inspiration for new you and other people, you will realize that it was all paid off, so don't get stuck on how things used to be. One of the most significant people in my life said: " If you can not be the last version you had been before you broke the rest of your life, then it was all worth it, whatever it was painful."

I once heard a quote:

" Every next level of your life will demand a new you. "
Sometimes, it takes being broken to become that new version of yourself.
